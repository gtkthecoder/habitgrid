Data Storage & Privacy

Local Storage

When you use HabitGrid without signing in, all your data is stored locally in your browser's storage. This means:

  • Your habits and progress are saved on your device only
  • No data is sent to any server
  • Data persists until you clear browser storage
  • You can export your data anytime

Google Drive Integration

When you sign in with Google, we use Google Drive to save your data. Here's how it works:

  • We create a single file called habitgrid-data.json in your Google Drive
  • Only this app can access this specific file
  • We request minimal permissions: only to create and update this one file
  • We cannot see or access any other files in your Google Drive
  • Your data syncs automatically when you're signed in

Google Account Information

When you sign in with Google, we only receive:

  • Your email address (to identify your account)
  • Your name and profile picture (optional, for display)
  • We do not store this information anywhere except locally on your device
  • We cannot access your Google password or other account details

No Server Storage

HabitGrid operates entirely on your device:

  • There is no backend server storing your data
  • All processing happens in your browser
  • We use no analytics or tracking services
  • The app works offline once loaded

Data Export & Control

You have full control over your data:

  • Export all data as JSON or CSV anytime
  • Delete your data by removing the Google Drive file or clearing browser storage
  • When signed in, you can disconnect the app from Google Drive in your Google Account settings
  • Local data is automatically backed up before overwriting

Cookies & Tracking

HabitGrid uses minimal technical storage:

  • Uses browser localStorage for app state
  • Uses sessionStorage for temporary data
  • No third-party cookies or tracking scripts
  • No advertising or marketing trackers

Open Source

HabitGrid is built with transparency in mind:

  • All code is viewable in your browser
  • Uses only standard web APIs
  • No hidden or minified tracking code
  • You can inspect network requests in your browser's developer tools

Questions?

If you have any questions about privacy or data handling, please:

  • Check the app's source code
  • Review Google's OAuth and Drive API documentation
  • Contact through the GitHub repository if available
